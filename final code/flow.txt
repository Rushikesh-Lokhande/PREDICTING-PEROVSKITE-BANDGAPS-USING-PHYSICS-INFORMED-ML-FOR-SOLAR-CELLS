## **The Master Research Flow: Physics-ML Integration**

### **Stage 1: Data Initialization & "Metadata" Cleaning**
* **Zero-Gap Partitioning:** Identify the 49.65% metallic population.
* **The "Paper" Extra:** Implement **Chemical Grouping**. Based on the papers, we will tag materials as "Oxides," "Halides," or "Chalcogenides." This allows the model to learn that physics changes based on the anion ($X$-site).

### **Stage 2: Advanced Physics-Informed Feature Engineering**
* **A. Hybrid Descriptors:** We won't just multiply features; we will create **Dimensionless Ratios**.
    * *Example:* $\frac{\chi_{diff}}{t}$ (Bonding energy per unit of geometric stability).
* **B. PCA for Phase Representation:** Use PCA on $a, b, c$ and Volume to create a **"Structural Phase Index."** This prevents the model from being redundant.
* **C. Stability Windowing:** Calculate the **Goldschmidt Tolerance Factor ($t$)** and **Octahedral Factor ($\mu$)**. 
    * *The "Paper" Extra:* Create a **Stability Heatmap** ($t$ vs $\mu$) to show where "Stable Perovskites" exist in your data.

### **Stage 3: Hierarchical "Dual-Stage" Training**
* **Step 1: Classification (Gatekeeper):** A model to predict "Metal (0eV) vs. Semiconductor (>0eV)." 
* **Step 2: Regression (Specialist):** A model trained **only** on semiconductors.
* **The "Paper" Extra: Data Balancing.** Since your semiconductor data has a "gap" between 2.0eV and 3.0eV, we will use **SMOTE for Regression** or **Weighted Loss Functions** to ensure the model doesn't just "ignore" rare wide-bandgap materials.

### **Stage 4: Hyperparameter Optimization (The "Grid Search" Push)**
* **Grid Search CV:** We will not guess parameters. We will run a 5-fold cross-validation grid search to optimize:
    * `Learning_rate`: To prevent the model from jumping over the "minimum error."
    * `Max_depth`: To capture the complex multi-site interactions (A-B-X).
    * `Subsample`: To make the model robust against noise in the DFT data.

### **Stage 5:  XAI (The "Physics Proof")**
* **A. Advanced SHAP:** We will generate **SHAP Interaction Plots**. This shows, for example, how B-site electronegativity affects the bandgap *differently* when the A-site radius is large versus small.
* **B. Mutual Information (MI) Scores:** We will use MI to prove that our non-linear model discovered physical relationships that a standard Pearson Heatmap missed.
* **C. The "Paper" Extra: Error Analysis.*Advanced* Plot "Error vs. Element Type." Do we fail more on Lead-based or Tin-based materials? This is critical for a Physics thesis.

### **Stage 6: The Meta-Learner (The Gold Standard)**
* **Model Stacking:** Combine **XGBoost** (the speed master), **CatBoost** (the categorical master), and **ExtraTrees** (the structural master).
* **Meta-Model:** Use a **Ridge Regressor** as the final "Judge" to produce the ultimate prediction.

### **Stage 7: Discovery & Band Alignment**
* **The Finale:** Scan the entire dataset to find the **Top 10 Materials**.
* **The "Paper" Extra: Band Alignment Prediction.** Use the **Mulliken Electronegativity theory** to predict where the Valence Band and Conduction Band sit relative to the vacuum level for your Top 10.
